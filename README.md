## 📌 Overview & My Role

This repository contains the curriculum, Jupyter Notebooks, and presentation materials developed for a 2-day interactive quantum computing workshop. 

As the **Founder of Zeta Science Forum and Lead Co-Organizer**, I orchestrated this event to bridge the gap between theoretical quantum mechanics and practical software implementation in Qiskit. My primary goal was to guide students and researchers from basic qubit manipulation into applied algorithmic problem-solving.

## 👨‍💻 My Instructional Focus: Qiskit Workflow & Optimization

During the workshop, I developed the curriculum for and taught the **Qiskit Workflow** and patterns session. My instruction focused on moving beyond basic circuit creation to build functional, hybrid quantum-classical algorithms.

* **Workflow Architecture:** Taught the standard Qiskit execution workflow (Map, Optimize, Execute, Post-process).
* **Algorithmic Case Study (MaxCut):** Guided participants through formulating the MaxCut problem as a Quadratic Unconstrained Binary Optimization (QUBO) model, mapping it to a quantum circuit, and executing the optimization routine using Qiskit.

## 📚 Complete Workshop Curriculum

The full workshop provided a comprehensive introduction to the quantum software pipeline, featuring hands-on sessions taught alongside my co-instructors:

1. **Fundamentals of Quantum Computing** *(Instructor: Shahzaib Abbas)*
   * Introduction to quantum mechanics, superposition, entanglement, and gate-level logic.
2. **Introduction to Qiskit** *(Instructor: Sheharyar Younis)*
   * Environment setup, circuit creation, statevector simulation, and measurement.
3. **Qiskit Workflow & Optimization** *(Instructor: Syed Ali Asghar)*
   * Implementation of hybrid algorithms and optimization case studies.
4. **The CHSH Inequality** *(Instructor: Lavish Kumar)* 
   * Mathematical implementation of Bell inequalities and analyzing quantum nonlocality via Qiskit.
5. **Advanced Algorithms & Applications**
   * Hands-on implementation of **Shor's Algorithm**.
   * Exploring the **Application of quantum computing in High Energy Physics**.
